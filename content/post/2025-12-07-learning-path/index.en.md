Have you ever asked yourself why, when we see successful people, we often only see them standing at the peak, but never the scars, scrapes, or worn-out shoes they endured on their journey up?

We are often instilled with a "linear path" mindset: if we want to excel at something, whether it's learning a new programming language, managing a team, or even practicing a sport, the process should be simple: `Start` -> `Learn` -> `Practice` -> `Succeed`.

But when we actually start doing it... why isn't it like that?
Why are there moments of discouragement? Why are there times when no matter what we do, it doesn't get better? Or are we just not good enough?

The short answer is... **"No, you're not inadequate. It's just that the map you're holding is wrong."**

Today, I'm here to lay out the "Reality" map for you.

## The Myth vs The Reality

![](post-image-en.png)

If you look at the infographic above, you'll see a clear difference.

*   **Left side (The Myth):** This is what we expect. We want the graph of life to shoot up in a straight 45-degree line, beautiful and predictable.
*   **Right side (The Reality):** This is what actually happens. It's messy, winding, and goes in circles, more like a maze than an escalator.

I want to invite everyone to understand the **8 crucial stages** you "must encounter" in real life, so we can prepare and deal with them like leaders.

---

## Delving into the 8 Stages of Learning (You Can't Avoid)

### 1. The Spark
Everything begins with passion or some necessity. During this period, our energy is at its highest, like when you've just started a new project or bought a new book.

### 2. The Struggle
Once you actually start... *"Wait, this isn't what we talked about!"* Difficulties begin to appear, self-doubt sets in. This is the first critical point where most people "give up."

### 3. The Learning Loop (Trial -> Learn -> Retry Loop)
This is the core. In the real world of work (especially in tech), we need to **Fail Fast**.
*   **Do:** Start coding, start managing.
*   **Learn:** See what the results are (they are often imperfect).
*   **Retry:** Use what you've learned to improve.

This loop is not a failure; it's about "tuning" the frequency to match the goal.

### 4. Failure as Feedback
Change your mindset: scars are not a mark of inadequacy, but data points telling you, *"This way isn't working. Try another path."*

### 5. The Plateau
This is the most important and often the most boring stage. It's when you're doing everything right, working just as hard, but the results seem stagnant. Many people quit here, thinking they're on the wrong path.

But the truth is, **"you are accumulating latent potential."** It's like water boiling; it looks still until it suddenly bubbles vigorously at the last minute.

### 6. The Pivot
When old methods no longer move us forward, we must be brave enough to stop, reassess, and change strategy. Stubbornness isn't always perseverance; rather, **flexibility** is the key to survival.

### 7. Small Wins
Don't wait to celebrate only at the finish line. Cherish the pride along the way. Successfully coding one function, completing a difficult task—these are excellent fuels.

### 8. Success as a New Beginning (The Goal as a New Start)
When you reach the peak, you'll discover the truth: there's a higher peak waiting ahead. Learning is not a "destination"; it is a "lifestyle."

> ## Embrace the Mess

![](hug.png)

As someone who works in development and management, I can confidently say that no project is as smooth as a bed of roses, and no skill is mastered overnight.

At the finish line, everyone might look the same—"successful." But what distinguishes a **"professional"** from an **"amateur"** is the ability to endure through stage 5 (The Plateau) and the courage to get back up during stage 3 (The Loop).

So, if your learning journey today is messy, confusing, or feels like you're going in circles... **Congratulations, you're on the right track!**

As long as you don't stop walking, this winding path will lead you to the most beautiful places.
